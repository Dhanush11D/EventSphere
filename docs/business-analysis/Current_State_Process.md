# Current State Business Process Analysis

## Project Information

**Project Name:** EventSphere Salesforce Implementation

**Sprint:** Sprint 1 – Discovery & Planning

**Scenario:** Scenario 2 – Business Process Discovery & Current-State Analysis

---

# Business Overview

EventSphere Pvt. Ltd. organizes more than 300 events every year across multiple cities. The company operates through several departments, including Sales, Event Operations, Marketing, Finance, and Customer Support.

Currently, each department manages its own work using spreadsheets, emails, and phone calls. This decentralized approach has resulted in duplicate data, manual processes, communication gaps, and delays in reporting.

The objective of this analysis is to understand the current ("As-Is") business process before designing a future Salesforce solution.
---

# Current Event Lifecycle (As-Is Process)

The current business process followed by EventSphere is:

1. Marketing announces a new event.
2. Event Operations creates an Excel spreadsheet to manage the event.
3. Finance creates a separate spreadsheet to track payments.
4. Attendee registrations are received through emails and phone calls.
5. Staff manually update attendee information in spreadsheets.
6. Speaker information is maintained in separate files.
7. Management requests weekly status reports.
8. Employees manually prepare PowerPoint presentations and reports for management.

# Existing Pain Points

- Event information is scattered across multiple spreadsheets.
- Duplicate attendee records are frequently created.
- Registration confirmation emails are sent manually.
- Event capacity is not validated automatically.
- There is no waiting list for full events.
- Speaker information is maintained separately from event details.
- Payment and refund tracking are manual.
- Weekly reports require significant manual effort.
- Management has no real-time dashboards or KPIs.
---

# Business Bottlenecks

- Event information is maintained in multiple Excel spreadsheets, resulting in inconsistent data.
- Departments work independently, leading to duplicate records and communication gaps.
- Attendee registrations require manual data entry and verification.
- Registration confirmation emails are sent manually, increasing processing time.
- Speaker information is not linked to event records, making coordination difficult.
- Payment and refund tracking are handled manually, increasing the risk of errors.
- Weekly reports are created manually, consuming significant employee time.
- Management cannot access real-time business insights due to the lack of dashboards.
---

# Automation Opportunities

The following business activities appear to be suitable candidates for automation in future project phases:

- Automate attendee registration confirmations.
- Validate event capacity during registration.
- Notify attendees about event schedule changes.
- Track speaker assignments and availability.
- Automate payment status updates and refund tracking.
- Generate real-time reports and dashboards for management.
- Send reminders for upcoming events to attendees and speakers.
- Reduce duplicate data entry by centralizing event information.
---
